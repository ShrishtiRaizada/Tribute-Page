Dr.A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

"Missile Man of India"

"Dreams are not what you see in sleep. They are the things that do not let you sleep".

  • Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalamwas born on 15 October 1931 to a Muslim family in the pilgrimage centre of Rameswaram,Tamil Nadu.
  • In 1955,he moved to Madras to study aerospace engineering in Madras Institute of Technology.
  • In 1960,Abdul Kalam joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of Defence Research and Development Organisation as a scientist.
  • In 1969,Kalam was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisationwhere he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).
  • In 1982,Kalam rejoined the Defence Research and Development Organisation and planned a program that produced a number of successful missiles,which helped earned him the nickname Missile Man.
  • In 1988,Kalam put forward a countrywide plan called Technology Vision 2020,which he described as a road map for transforming India from a less-developed to a developed society in 20 years.
  • In 2002,Kalam sworn in as India's 11th President.
  • On 27 July 2015,Kalam travelled to Shillong to deliver a lecture on "Creating a Livable Planet Earth"at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.After 5 minutes into his lecutre he felt some discomfort and collapsed.At 7:45 pm he was confirmed dead due to sudden cardiac arrest.

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was a professor,author,politician and one of the greatest aerospace scientist.He is also a recepient of popular awards like Bharat Ratna,Padma Bhushan and Padma Vibushan.Some of his famous books are Wings of Fire,India 2020 and Ignited Minds.
